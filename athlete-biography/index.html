Early Life


From Cheltenham to the Slopes

On December the 5th Michael David Edwards (AKA Eddie the Eagle), was born in the town of Cheltenham, Gloucestershire, U.K.

His school friends called him "Eddie", based on his surname Edwards. At the age of 13 his journey to become "The Eagle" began.

This was due to a school ski trip causing him to develop an enthusiasm for the sport, it would last a lifetime and change his destiny.

He honed his skills on the slopes through the Gloucestershire Ski and Snowboard Centre. He competed in Alpine skiing and ski slalom.

His career expanded to include competition abroad. After losing the opportunity to compete in Sarajevo in 1984, he decided to change focus.

According to BBC Sports (2026), Eddie's switch to ski jumping was also motivated by financial reasons. It was £3.50/day, skiing £200.
